Tolerances, fits, and limits

Limits and fits for shafts and holes; clearance, transition, and interference classes. Why a bearing seat tolerance is a maintenance issue, not a design nicety. Reading fit callouts on real drawings.

Primary textMIT OCW / NPTEL: Engineering Drawing and Com
Assessmentworked examples + checkpoint questions

Lecture

After this lesson you can answer:
  • An H7/g6 callout: clearance, transition, or interference — and what duty suits it?
  • Why is a bearing-seat fit a maintenance concern rather than a design nicety?

§1 · Tolerance is a purchase

No process makes exact dimensions; tolerance is how much inexactness the function can afford. Tightening a tolerance buys precision with money — grinding instead of turning, gauging instead of measuring. The engineer's discipline is to tolerance functionally: as loose as the function allows, as tight as it demands, feature by feature. Blanket tight tolerances are a tax the shop invoices back.

§2 · The ISO fit system

Cylindrical fits are standardized as a letter-number pair on each member: the letter locates the tolerance zone (A…H clearance side for holes, h…z for shafts), the number (IT grade) sets its width. The hole-basis convention keeps the hole at H (zone starting at nominal) and moves the shaft:

\[ \text{H7/g6: running clearance} \qquad \text{H7/k6: transition} \qquad \text{H7/p6: interference} \tag{1} \]

For ⌀45 H7/g6: hole 45.000/45.025, shaft 44.991/44.975 — clearance between 0.009 and 0.050 mm: a shaft that turns freely with minimal slop, the classic bearing-journal recipe.

§3 · Reading limits like a fitter

Every fit resolves to four numbers — hole max/min, shaft max/min — and two extremes: tightest (min hole, max shaft) and loosest (max hole, min shaft). Compute both before assembly decisions: will it press, slide, or rattle? Bearing catalogs, coupling manuals, and seal datasheets all speak this language; the fitter's micrometer settles the argument.

Closing. Tolerances price function; the ISO letter-number system encodes fits compactly; and four limit numbers per joint tell you tight, loose, and everything a rebuild needs to know.

Foundations

What this lesson assumes

  • Dimensioning (Lesson 5) — limits attach to dimensions.
  • Decimal discipline — thousandths of a millimetre matter here.

Glossary

  • IT grade — tolerance-zone width class (IT6 tighter than IT8).
  • Hole basis — hole held at H; shaft letter sets the fit.
  • Clearance/transition/interference — always loose / could be either / always tight.
  • Allowance — the tightest intended condition of a fit.
  • Limits — the max and min values a dimension may take.

Worked Examples

Worked example 1 — decode ⌀45 H7/g6 completely

Problem. For ⌀45 H7/g6 (hole 45.000/45.025; shaft 44.991/44.975), find the extreme clearances and classify the fit.

Solution. (1) Tightest: min hole − max shaft = 45.000 − 44.991 = 0.009 mm. (2) Loosest: max hole − min shaft = 45.025 − 44.975 = 0.050 mm. (3) Both positive → pure clearance fit; magnitudes (9–50 µm on ⌀45) → precision running fit.

Answer: clearance 0.009–0.050 mm; precision running fit (journals, sliding hubs). Sanity check: clearance ≈ 0.2–1‰ of diameter — the classic oil-film neighborhood.

Worked example 2 — will it press or slide?

Problem. A coupling hub is bored ⌀60 H7 (60.000/60.030); the shaft end measures 60.021 on site. Find: the assembly condition across the hole's range.

Solution. (1) Against min hole: 60.000 − 60.021 = −0.021 → 21 µm interference. (2) Against max hole: 60.030 − 60.021 = +0.009 → 9 µm clearance. (3) The joint is transition: this specific hub may press on or slip on depending on its actual bore — measure the hub, then decide heating or pressing.

Answer: between 21 µm tight and 9 µm loose — measure the mating bore before method selection. Sanity check: transition fits exist exactly for keyed couplings: snug location, feasible assembly; the k6 shaft zone straddling nominal is the giveaway.

Kuwait Floor

Vignette — the bearing that spun its seat. You arrive at a repeated bearing failure: inner races spinning on a fan shaft, blued and scored. Site measurements tell the story the work orders didn't — the shaft journal, polished by three "emergency" rebuilds, sits 30 µm under its k5 zone: what should be a light transition fit became running clearance under load. The metallurgy is fine; the fit died. Repair by metal-spray and regrind back to limits, and the failure mode disappears. The lesson you brief the crew: a bearing seat is a toleranced dimension with a job, and every polish "just to clean it up" spends the fit.
